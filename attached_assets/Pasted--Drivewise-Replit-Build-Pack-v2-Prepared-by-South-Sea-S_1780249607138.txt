# Drivewise Replit Build Pack (v2)

Prepared by South Sea Solutions.

This is the complete development prompt set for building the production Drivewise platform on Replit. Paste the prompts into the Replit Agent in order, one at a time, and confirm each step works before moving to the next. Prompt 0 is the master context: paste it first, and re-paste it any time the Agent drifts off the rules.

Style rule for every file the Agent produces: no em dashes anywhere. Use commas, periods, colons, parentheses, or a middot. This applies to UI copy, comments, and documentation.

---

## What Drivewise is (read before building)

Drivewise is a driver-risk and certification platform for commercial road transport in Botswana, starting with the Gaborone and Francistown corridors. It is not an electronic logging device for legal compliance. Botswana has no statutory driving-hours limit, no ELD mandate, and no national demerit-point system. The forces that make operators buy are private: mines, large shippers, and cross-border clients that impose safety and fatigue requirements on the hauliers they contract, and insurers that price premiums on driver risk. Drivewise lets a haulier prove its drivers are safe and certified, win and keep those contracts, and reduce crash and fuel losses.

There are two surfaces with two audiences:

1. The driver app (phone-first, installable). The driver sets duty status, sees fatigue and hours clocks, builds a daily log, and certifies it. English and Setswana. This is the engagement wedge.
2. The operator platform (web). The office sees a fleet view, alerts, and a per-driver record (overview, hours, safety, training and certification, documents). This is the oversight and sales tool.

The data comes from the phone (location, speed, distance) and from driver self-report (fuel, odometer). There is no engine telematics hardware. Do not build engine telemetry.

The recurring revenue and the moat is certification: a Drivewise Certified Driver credential with courses, scores, and annual validity, plus a fleet certified percentage that operators take into tenders and insurer reviews.

## Architecture

- Framework: Next.js (App Router) with TypeScript.
- Styling: Tailwind CSS. Design tokens below.
- Database: PostgreSQL with Prisma ORM (Replit provides a Postgres add-on).
- Auth: email and password with role-based access (Owner, Operator, Driver). Session via secure HTTP-only cookies.
- Driver app: a Progressive Web App (installable, works on a low-end Android phone, tolerant of patchy signal, queues writes when offline and syncs when back online).
- Internationalisation: English and Setswana only, with a simple key-based dictionary and English fallback.
- Hosting and data residency: deploy in the closest available region and document it. Personal and certificate data are minimal by design under the Botswana Data Protection Act, 2024. Every read of a driver record writes to an audit log.

## Data model (target Prisma schema)

The Agent will generate this in Prompt 2. It is included here so every later prompt can refer to it.

```
Org            id, name, region, createdAt
User           id, orgId, email, passwordHash, role (OWNER|OPERATOR|DRIVER), name, locale, driverId?
Fleet          id, orgId, name, depot
Vehicle        id, fleetId, reg, active
Driver         id, orgId, name, employeeNo, dob, phone, languages, homeDepot,
               emergencyContact, licenceNo, licenceClass, prdp, currentVehicleId?,
               safetyScore (int), onTimePct (int)
RuleProfile    id, orgId, name, contMins, dailyMins, weeklyMins, dailyRestMins, breakMins, isDefault
                 (defaults: cont 270, daily 540, weekly 3360, dailyRest 660, break 45)
DutyEvent      id, driverId, status (OFF|REST|DRIVING|ONDUTY), startedAt, endedAt?, source (PHONE|MANUAL)
DailyLog       id, driverId, date, driveMins, dutyMins, restMins, certifiedAt?, certified (bool)
TripPing       id, driverId, vehicleId, lat, lng, speedKph, placeLabel, recordedAt   (from phone GPS)
FuelReport     id, driverId, vehicleId, odometerKm, fuelPct, litres?, recordedAt      (driver entered)
Incident       id, driverId, type, detail, severity (LOW|MEDIUM|HIGH), status (OPEN|RESOLVED),
               riskPoints (int), occurredAt
Document       id, driverId, type (LICENCE|PRDP|MEDICAL_CERT|DEFENSIVE_DRIVING|OTHER),
               label, expiresOn                 (medical is a validity date only, no clinical data)
Course         id, orgId, title, ordinal
CourseCompletion id, driverId, courseId, status (DONE|IN_PROGRESS|TODO), score?, completedOn?
Certification  id, driverId, status (CERTIFIED|IN_PROGRESS|LAPSED), issuedOn?, expiresOn?
AuditLog       id, orgId, actorUserId, action, subjectType, subjectId, at
```

Derived values (compute, do not store): fleet certified percentage, needs-attention flags, fatigue and hours clock positions, document and certificate state (valid, due soon within 60 days, expired).

```

### Prompt 4: fatigue and hours engine, test first

```

first using the cases in the build pack, then implement until they pass. The engine
must be configurable by RuleProfile and must carry a code comment that these limits

## Fatigue and hours engine test cases (for Prompt 4)

Defaults: continuous 270, daily 540, weekly 3360, daily rest 660, break 45 (minutes).

1. No driving today returns continuous 0, daily 0, status OK.
2. 240 minutes continuous driving with no qualifying break returns WARNING (at or above 90 percent of 270).
3. 270 minutes continuous driving returns EXCEEDED on the continuous limit.
4. 200 minutes driving, then a 45 minute REST, then 60 minutes driving returns continuous 60, not 260 (the break reset the continuous count).
5. 200 minutes driving, then a 30 minute REST, then 60 minutes driving returns continuous 260 (the break was shorter than 45 minutes and did not qualify).
6. Daily driving of 486 minutes returns WARNING (at or above 90 percent of 540).
7. Daily driving of 540 minutes returns EXCEEDED on the daily limit.
8. Weekly driving of 3360 minutes returns EXCEEDED on the weekly limit, even if the daily and continuous counts are OK.

Each result must include minutes remaining on every limit, and the overall status is the most severe of the three limit statuses.

## What NOT to build (the fat we already cut)

- No engine telemetry of any kind (RPM, engine temperature, oil pressure, AdBlue, fault codes, satellites, signal strength). Phone GPS and driver input only.
- No clinical medical fields. Medical is a certificate validity date.
- No demerit-point system or any framing of points as a legal licence status.
- No languages beyond English and Setswana.
- No decorative map until there is real GPS to plot. If a map is added later, it must plot actual TripPings.
- No claim, anywhere in copy, that Drivewise satisfies a Botswana legal driving-hours or ELD requirement. The standard is contractual.

## Acceptance criteria for the whole build

- A driver installs the app, sets status, sees live clocks, enters fuel, and certifies a day, including offline then syncing.
- An operator sees the fleet view, acts on alerts, and opens any driver record across Overview, Hours, Safety, Training, and Documents.
- Completing courses moves a driver to Certified and updates the fleet certified percentage.
- Document and certificate expiries drive alerts.
- Every driver-record access is audit-logged, and drivers cannot reach each other's data.
- English and Setswana both work with clean fallback.
- No em dashes anywhere. No engine telemetry. No clinical medical data. No demerit system.
